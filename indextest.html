DWARVES
Stout, honor-driven, decisive, and gifted craftsmen. (p19)

DWARF TRAITS (p21)
All Dwarves have improved vision in low light/darkness, skill in a trade, training with a dwarven weapon, and are less vunerable to poisoning and resistant against poison damage

ELVES
Agile, enthralling, keen-eyed, and sharp-minded. Frequently act with aloofness or indiference towards other races, especially shorter-lived ones like Humans and Dragonborn. Not uncommon for them to get along well with Halflings and Gnomes, or to have a respectfully adversarial relationship with Dwarves. (p22)

ELF TRAITS (p24)
All Elves have improved vision in low light/darkness, heightened senses of awareness, reduced vulnerability to mind altering magic effects, and require only 4 hours of meditation per day instead of sleep.

High Elf (p24)
Secluded, and sometimes completely isolationist. Typically cordial and gracious, but almost universally arrogant.


+2 DEX  +1 INT

Wood Elf (p25)
Secluded, reclusive, and highly suspicious of all non-Elves. Shown respect, they tend on the whole to be extraordinarily decent and willing to cooperate for the greater good.

HALFLINGS
Nimble, courageous, charmingly modest, and oddly lucky. Respected for their overall goodness and lack of complication as a people, but often as easily overlooked for their small stature as their seeming lack of ambition. In truth, they're drawn to simplicity by nature. The heart of the halfling lies with simple pleasures, and suffering for more than that seems pointless. (p27)

HALFLING TRAITS (p29)
All Halflings can move easily through crowds, dart around and under most things larger than 4 feet tall, and are less vulnerable to being frightened.

Lightfooted (p29)
Most common. Friendly and helpful by nature. Curious, usually polite, and eager to travel.

HUMANS
Jacks-of-all-trades. All at once unexceptional and impressive. Commonly looked down upon by Elves and Dwarves for their mayfly lifespans (500-700 years vs 90 years), but almost universally respected as a people for their tenacity and accomplishment in the face of it. (p30)

Humans
Humans present by far the broadest variation of cultures, individual personalities, and interests. (p31-32)

GNOMES
Dangerously clever, impulsive, outgoing, and enthusiastic. Often irresponsibile, but overwhelming good-natured as a people. Also small as fuck. (p36)

Gnome Traits (p37-38)
All Gnomes have improved vision in low light/darkness, and reduced vulnerability to certain types of magic.

Forest Gnome (p38 left)
Tricksy and elusive. Riddles and shit. Nature-dwelling pranksters. Often friendly with wood elves.
